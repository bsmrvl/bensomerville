Chartree - Growing Trees of Character (Not Exactly Data Science)
Grow your own trees on a monospace grid. Choose the material (unicode) for your tree as well as the hue of the sky (also unicode). Heaps of other parameters as well!

Illustrating Political Subtlety through Machine Learning
You can guess a lot about a person by their political opinions. Take any question—what kind of job does this person have?—how educated are they?—where did they grow up? Instead of asking them directly, ask them 40 political questions. You'll get an idea about your unasked Q, oh yes you will. The thing is, you'll probably be wrong.

Connecting Demographics to Political Opinions in the Post-Spring Arab World
Arab Barometer is an organization which has conducted five giant surveys across the Arab world since 2006, asking participants around 300 questions about how they live and what they think. The data is available for free, and the possibilities for analysis seem endless. I lived in Tunisia from 2012-13 as an uninterested highschooler, so I wanted to revisit that era and chose to investigate AB's third wave of surveys, covering 2012-14.

The Somerville Weekly
Vol. 1, Iss. 2
What's going on here? It may look like a humble gig, but in fact it was an all-expenses paid trip to a luxury resort on the coast of Turkey. Early one morning Chris (right) went out and took the picture below, just as I was finally headed to sleep (why?).
Ben, circa January 2014.
Yes, that's me – awoken from daytime slumber only to provide thirty minutes of thump, eager to return to nocturnalism. (side note: proficiency on the cajon is never overlooked, and it's amazing where it can take you. If you're gonna master just one instrument, I recommend the cajon!)
Anyway, what on earth was I doing up til sunrise? Tempting as it was to throw back free mocktails all night, that wasn't it. I was actually sitting on my balcony quite sober, swerving the 8-hour jetlag by retaining my east-coast sleep schedule, ready to laugh at my bandmates who worked so hard to adapt to Turkey time only to be miserable again on return.

But I wasn't actively thinking about that at the time. No, I was making good use of my strange hours, scribbling some nonsense which would soon become iconic. No matter how much you look at blue-hoodied adolescent above and think "how could any words from his pen be any good?"–and no matter how correct that may be... These words will remain historic. Because these words belong to the first song on my first album (click them to listen!).
Already I apologize for misleading– unfortunately the first scribbling was not in calligraphy. In fact, I'm pretty sure I was sitting on the balcony typing on my iPod touch.

These, I wrote out today, just over six years after that original midnight Mediterranean writing session. They belong to Manna, track 1 of The Greenstone EP (2015).
Here it is, a relic. Some of you readers are here because I bribed you with a free CD. That's because when I finished recording it in December 2015, I thought a few Facebook shares would be enough to sell a thousand copies. Well, I won't tell you how many I have left... Let's just say if all of you were to order 10 copies for your friends (which I won't stop you from doing–seriously, click the image above to order!), I still wouldn't be out.

But that's not the point. The reason I spend all this time talking about an old project is that it never got its deserved attention. It's honestly genius, and I'm ashamed I never put in the effort to get it heard. Of course it's immature, I was 17! And what's wrong with that? It may not describe the human condition with sweeping splendor, but it sure describes something. Something adolescent and angsty–things I try not to be anymore, but things that are real.

I'm done acting holier-than-thou to my past self and refusing to own up to my creation. This album is awesome! Show it to your angsty nieces and brothers-in-law!
P.S. – I may be giving away some personalized poems, handwritten like the above Manna lyrics and mailed to your door. Stay tuned for that. And thanks for reading!

-Ben

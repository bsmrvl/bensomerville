Let us go backwards! The above gif is from a real recording session in April 2017, in which I trekked probably half a mile through the woods to this tunnel, with all my recording gear, just to get a lead vocal track for The Stage. I ended up only using a small clip from this session, somewhere in the final chorus where all the vocals start to come in (go to 2:46).

All of Entanglement was recorded this way, onto a battery-powered recorder, in strange places away from home. I mentioned a few weeks ago that most of it was in the back of my car. But there were also a fair amount of these more interesting places--acoustically, for one thing--but also in the effect they had on me while I sang and played. 

The effect wasn't always helpful. The reason I hardly used any of the track recorded that day was that the euphoria was too strong. As you may be able to tell from the gif-- the hike and spring air, the strange reverberations of passing cars above, the feeling of probably being the first person ever to record a vocal track in this nostalgic culvert --it got me pretty excited, too excited.. When you do passionate work, you want your soul to be simmering, but not boiling over!

But, though that rendered it mostly useless, it did add something perfect to that final chorus of The Stage, at which the song itself is writhing to surge past the boiling point.
 

Speaking of the song itself, what in the world is it about? Well, if you've listened at all to Entanglement, you'll know it's a narrative (or the partially excavated fossil of one -- just wait til the full novel comes out!).

To make it as confusing as possible (classic 2015-18 era Ben), I gave this story several narrators, without clearly differentiating between them. The Stage is the first track narrated by the central character (referred to as M in the notes below), but you wouldn't know that unless I told you directly, as I just did.

But I'm not gonna give you an entire outline right now! If you'd like, it's stated vaguely on the album page.

For now I'd like to show you some of the original notes, long before the song hit final form. Let the pink comments guide you!
REBUILDING (2199-2212, M 18)    this story occurs at a very particular time in the future, for no particular reason
 
[ older generation increasingly apathetic about future
 
the youth of the land (teenage M&co and “gov”) take things into their own hands, working side by side to rebuild, gov wasn’t gov at the time ]    line up pretty directly with the final lyrics
 
[ gradually separate: M&co takes the hard labor while “gov” shifts to administration, commanding M&co to provide what she needs in order to improve society
 
still very dependent on each other despite their growing frustration ]    very important, but ended up being very mysterious and chaotic in the final
 
weird tuning volta esque fast groove    refers to the original musical idea which became the music of this song -- listen below!!

”the good chant” melody over chorus
 
creating the machine - passion, [ selfishness    reply and finish the line for bonus points! 
 
once upon a long, at the turn of the cent
we hear our future drift over the crest
all the elders crying, and they drag us along
we don’t know better than to join in song
but not for long
(were they wrong?)
 
a couple of us gather at the age of ten
we jibe at this idea of a crest
let the elders cry, let them cry
and let us formulate our own lie

October 2016
If that's all a bunch of gibberish, I get it. This has been a pretty wordy issue as is! But I always find it fascinating to look back on the original seeds of ideas.

The "weird tuning volta esque fast groove" refers to the musical idea which I had recorded months, if not years prior, with no intent to turn into a full song. It's quite different, and has an energy which I sometimes wish I had captured a little better in the final version. It's also very rough and rambling. But I will let it conclude this history for now.

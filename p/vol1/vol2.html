BEN SOMERVILLE

The Somerville Weekly

Volume II, Issue I

Turned on our equator dime
And look! the weekly's due
Sorentino marked departure
Four twenty-five, year of doom

Released no single, no song
But drove through many places
Learned to wrangle the WWW
And hammered ink on pages

Now and always

There's a rhythm where I walk
There's a rhythm where I walk

Beneath the clear southern sky
Where people like to breathe
There's music in the cafes at night
Commotion of the beat
Yes now and always

There's the gift of many ages
The gift of many kinds
Bring your gift up the hill, they said
Leave it somewhere we can find!

There's a rhythm where I walk
There's a rhythm where I walk
There's a rhythm where I skip and run
There's a rhythm where I walk



---Ben
